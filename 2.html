Interesting Early Robots

“Man will have become to the machine what the horse and the dog are to man.” — Samuel Butler

Humans have experimented with making machines which can imitate the work that humans do. But the invention of robots, as we know them now, really took off only in the later part of the 20th century. Numerous robots , or to give the proper term ‘automata’ were created before the 20th. These were more of decorative or recreational purposes than for constructive work. The word Automaata comes from a Greek word meaning ‘acting on one’s own will’. Few automata constructed before the sixteenth century have survived, but their existence was recorded in books and manuscripts .

1. 10 CE - 70 CE

The Greek engineer, Heron or Hero of Alexandria was one of the finets inventors of his time. He produced two tests – the Pneumatica and the Automata, in which he describes many of his invesntions. The Aeloipil or (or aeolipyle, or eolipile), also known as a Hero's engine, was one of his most famous invesntions. The Aeolopile consists of a simple bladeless radial steam turbine which spins when the central water container is heated. Torque is produced by steam jets exiting the turbine, much like a tip jet or rocket engine. It is considered to be the first recorded steam engine or reaction steam turbine. Other important inventions by Heron were a coin operated Holy Water Dispenser, a surveying device known as the Dioptra, Heron’s fountain which used air pressure to produce a jet of water and a primitive programmable automaton or robot.

2. 725 CE

First true mechanical clock – At the time of teh Tang Dynasty, Chinese engineer Liang Lingzan and Buddhist monk Yi Xing build a water-driven device with the world's first clockwork escapement mechanism – the first true mechanical clock

3. 1495 CE

Da Vinci’s Robotic Knight – Leonardo Da Vinci’s robotic knight was on of the most famous inventions of the middle ages. Designed for a pageant in Milan by order of his patron the Duke Lodovico Sforza, the Robotic Knight consistd of a knigh suit filled with gears and wheels connected to a cable and pulley system. These helped the Knight to be capable of independent motion - it could stand, sit, raise its visor and independently manoeuver its arms, and had an anatomically correct jaw.

4. 1810

The Kaufmann Trumpet Player –The Kaufmann Trumpet Player is one of the first humanoid robots invented which has survived to our time. Made by the German inventor, Friedich Kaufmann, the model , of about 80 cms in height , is that of a man in Spanish uniform. It holds a trumpet in its right hand which it raises to its mouth from time to time and blows on it, imitating the sound of a brass instrument. It has leather bellows for lungs which are powered by a spring mechanism that need to be wound up by a hand crank on the right. It is at present, in the Deutsches Museum, München.
