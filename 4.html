AIBO

AIBO ( short for , Artificial Intelligence Robot) and also "pal" or "partner" in Japanese, is a part of a series of robotic pets. Sony announced a prototype Aibo in mid-1998. But the the first AIBO was released by Sony 11th th May 1999 . Aibo was an entertainment robotic dog, designed to be an intelligent and trainable companion. Upgraded versions were released every year until 2006. The company stopped releasing ne Aibo versions in 2006 in an effort to try to control costs and pare losses in its electronics businesses.

Building of newer versions restarted in 2017 and the upgraded Aibo featuring improved AI (artificial intelligence) software as well as other technologies was made available in 2018. The version ERS-110 was the first Aibo released to the market and had a beagle like appearance with a silver and golden-brown hue and grey claws. It could walk smoothly on four legs as well as perform other complex motions. The Aibo had the ability to learn, entertain and communicate with its owner. It responded to voice commands by its owner through a tone-based remote control unit. It could show emotions such as happiness and anger and instincts such as the need for companionship. It could operate autonomously in response to external stimuli.

The later versions were more developed with a better ability to respond and play with the owners. The latest version of Aibo was released in February, 2019. The The ERS-1000, or the The “Choco Edition” gives Aibo a coat in two lovely shades of brown, retaining the white legs and paws. It is cuter than the earlier Aibos and can be programmed by its owner with a web-based API . It can recognize up to 100 faces, and can memorize a customize-able trick. Speech-recognition software lets it learn and respond to about 50 commands. It can recognize its owner's face, detect smiles and words of praise, and learn new tricks. In addition to all the usual tricks like coming to heel or chasing a ball, it can also do a little dance and wave a front paw on hearing its name. But the Aibo does not come cheap. It costs 198,000 yen in Japan, or about $1,800, with a 2,980-yen ($27)-a-month plan on top for LTE connectivity and cloud services.
